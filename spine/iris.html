— The visual identity the book wears — every color, type style, and component the pages put on. A biometric in the same sense the book argues for: pattern inscribed in flesh, recognizable on sight. All the page styles below come from the same styles.css; this catalog is just the look at what's in it.

character

What this design is and isn't. The rules that hold the look together.

the page is a reader, not a product

Every choice goes toward readability and disappearance. The design holds the text — it doesn't compete with it. No hero sections. No cards. No shadows. No conversion logic.

hierarchy through type, not decoration

Size, weight, family, and color do all the structural work. The hairline rule (1px solid var(--rule)) is the only divider treatment. There are no icons in the prose, no illustrations, no background textures.

monochrome with one earned register

Black, off-white, grey, and the browser's default link blue. The phosphor green (--green / #00AA00) earns its place as the color of a terminal cursor — the thing that blinks and waits for input. It appears in exactly one register: the membrane disclosure — the $ prefix on each rule and the chevron on the toggle. Outside it, nothing else gets a color.

honest about its state

The work-in-progress badge isn't a disclaimer. It's a design decision. The site knows what it is and says so plainly. Finished-looking polish would be a lie.

static — except for the eye

The book pages are static. No animations, no transitions, no motion — the reading surface should disappear. Iris is the one place that breaks the rule: the eye watches the cursor, shrinks when you scroll past it, and fades when its footer twin appears. The page that catalogs the biometric wears one.

found by link, not by search

Robots are blocked. There is no SEO. The site reaches people because someone sent them the URL. That's intentional — consistent with the book's argument that authenticated, directed intent is more meaningful than ambient discovery.

buttons

One button style across the site — neutral .copy with a two-line label. The index uses it to copy the whole spine; each unit page uses it to copy its own focus payload. No inverted/primary variant — the bar above (membrane) is the only dark element on the page.

navigation

Per-unit pages frame the chapter with a centered close-X + "theLastInterface" mini-title at the top, a prev/next row at the bottom, and another close-X below that. The X is the only "back to the spine" affordance.

.page-top — centered .page-close (40×40 hit area, 24px icon). Default shows the X; on hover the X collapses to a point and a circle blooms out (~0.25s). One continuous gesture.

spacing

Six-step scale defined in :root. All structural margin and padding uses these tokens. Component micro-values (≤14px) stay hardcoded.

--space-xs 8px — tight internal gaps
--space-s 16px — compact spacing
--space-m 24px — standard delimiter
--space-l 40px — loose gap
--space-xl 48px — section break
--space-2xl 96px — page-level padding

mobile (≤640px)

A single @media (max-width: 640px) block in styles.css tightens padding, scales type down a touch, and lets the prev/next nav stack vertically when chapter titles would collide.
